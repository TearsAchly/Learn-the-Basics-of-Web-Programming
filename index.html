Bandung

The largest metropolitan city in West Java Province, it also serves as the provincial capital.

history

The word Bandung comes from the word "bendung" or "dam," referring to the damming of the Citarum River by the lava of Mount Tangkuban Parahu, which then formed a lake. A legend told by Bandung's elders says that the name Bandung comes from a watercraft consisting of two boats tied side by side, called a "bangdung boat," used by the Regent of Bandung, R.A. Wiranatakusumah II, to navigate the Citarum River in search of a new regency seat to replace the old capital in Dayeuhkolot.

Based on Sundanese philosophy, the word Bandung also comes from the phrase Nga-Bandung-an Banda Indung, which is a sacred and sublime phrase because it embodies Sundanese teachings. "Nga-Bandung-an" means to witness or to bear witness. Banda refers to everything in the natural world, namely the earth and the atmosphere, both living and inanimate. A synonym for banda is treasure. Indung means Mother or Earth, also known as Mother Earth, where Banda is located.

geographical

The city of Bandung is surrounded by mountains, so its morphology resembles a giant bowl. Geographically, the city is located in the center of West Java province, at an altitude of approximately 768 meters above sea level. The highest point is in the north at 1,050 meters above sea level, and the lowlands are in the south at 675 meters above sea level.

Bandung is crossed by two main rivers: the Cikapundung and the Citarum, along with their tributaries, which generally flow south and converge into the Citarum River. This makes South Bandung highly susceptible to flooding, especially during the rainy season.

tour

Since the opening of the Cipularang Toll Road, Bandung has become a prime weekend getaway destination, especially for people from the surrounding Jakarta area. Besides being a shopping destination, Bandung is also known for its numerous old buildings with Dutch architectural heritage.

Farm House Lembang

Located on the main Bandung-Lembang route, Farm House is a tourist attraction that's always crowded with visitors. Besides its strategic location, this area also offers a distinctly European tourist atmosphere. All of this is done in the form of Instagrammable selfie spots.

Observatorium Bosscha

It houses several telescopes, including the Zeiss Double Refractor, Schmidt Milky Way Telescope, Bamberg Refractor, GOTO Cassegrain Telescope, and Solar Telescope. The Zeiss Double Refractor is the largest telescope for stargazing. It is mounted on a dome roof, requiring the roof to be opened when the telescope is in use. Anyone can visit the Bosscha Observatory without a ticket. However, registration is required for those wishing to use the Zeiss telescope. Educational institutions and agencies are available from Tuesday to Friday. Individual visits are available every Saturday.
